OUR WORK

Problem definition and Explanation:

Terrace greenhouses can provide a great opportunity for people to grow their own fresh produce and plants, but they can also come with some challenges. Here are some problem statements that people might face in terrace greenhouses

    1) Temperature control: Terrace greenhouses can get very hot during the day and very cold at night, especially if they don't have proper insulation or ventilation. This can be challenging for plants that require a specific temperature range to thrive.

    2) Watering and irrigation: Proper watering and irrigation can be a challenge in terrace greenhouses, especially if they don't have a water source nearby or if the terrace has limited drainage. Overwatering or underwatering can be detrimental to plants, so it's important to find the right balance.

    3) Limited space: Terrace greenhouses are often smaller than traditional greenhouses, which can make it challenging to grow a large variety of plants or to accommodate larger plants. It's important to plan carefully and choose plants that are well-suited for the available space.

    4) Structural integrity: Terrace greenhouses need to be sturdy enough to withstand wind, rain, and other weather conditions. It's important to ensure that the greenhouse is built to withstand the specific environmental conditions in the area and that it is regularly inspected for any signs of wear or damage.

    5) Limited sunlight: Depending on the location and orientation of the terrace, it may receive limited sunlight, which can be a challenge for plants that require a lot of light to grow. It's important to choose plants that are well-suited for the available light conditions and to consider adding artificial lighting if necessary.



Solution to the addressed probem!

An AIOT-based greenhouse monitoring system is a system that combines Artificial Intelligence (AI) and the Internet of Things (IoT) technologies to monitor and control various parameters in a greenhouse environment. The system typically consists of a network of sensors and devices that collect data on various parameters such as temperature, humidity, light, soil moisture, and plant growth. These sensors transmit data to a central hub or server, where the data is processed and analyzed using AI algorithms. Based on the analysis, the system can automatically control the environmental conditions in the greenhouse by adjusting parameters such as temperature, humidity, and light to create optimal conditions for plant growth. This can include controlling heating and cooling systems, turning on or off lights, and adjusting the amount of water provided to the plants. The system can also provide alerts to the user if any parameters fall outside of their predetermined ranges. For example, if the temperature in the greenhouse becomes too high, the system can send an alert to the user's phone or email, allowing them to take appropriate act. Overall, an AIOT-based greenhouse monitoring system can help growers optimize their crop yields, reduce energy usage, and minimize the need for manual intervention



Outcomes!

This automated greenhouse can offer a more efficient, productive, and profitable way of growing crops.
● Consistent quality: With precise control over growing conditions, crops can be grown to consistent standards, resulting in higher quality produce.
● Reduced labour costs: Automation can reduce the need for manual labour, which can save on labour costs and improve efficiency.
● Efficient use of resources: By using technology to optimise growing conditions,an automated greenhouse can use resources more efficiently, reducing waste and saving money.
● Better pest and disease management: Automation can help to detect and control pests and diseases more effectively, reducing the risk of crop loss.
